Bali Tips for Solo Women

ğŸ›µ Scooter Safety

Traffic is the #1 danger in Bali. If you're not an experienced rider, use Grab/Gojek or hire a driver. Scooter accidents are common and medical facilities are limited.

ğŸ¸ Drink Awareness

Drink spiking and methanol poisoning (in cheap alcohol) are real risks, especially in Kuta and party areas. Stick to sealed bottles, watch your drinks, and choose reputable venues.

Cover shoulders and knees at temples. Sarongs are usually provided or can be rented. Don't enter if menstruatingâ€”it's a religious rule, not discrimination.

ğŸ‘™ Beach Dress

Bikinis are fine at tourist beaches but cover up when leaving the beach area. Bali is more conservative than it appears in Instagram posts.

Use ATMs inside banks to avoid skimmers. Negotiate prices at markets but respect fair exchange. Many places now take cards, but cash is still king for small vendors.
