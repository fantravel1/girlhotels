Packing Light: The Solo Woman's Guide

How to fit two weeks into a carry-on, move freely through cities, and never wait at baggage claim again.

Why Packing Light Changes Everything

Here's the truth: heavy luggage makes you a target. Struggling with a massive suitcase through a train station, flagging down help to lift it onto a bus, standing exhausted at baggage claim while everyone else breezes through‚Äîit's not just inconvenient, it's a safety issue. Light packing gives you freedom, speed, and independence.

I've traveled for three weeks across five countries with nothing but a 35L backpack. Here's how you can too.

The Golden Rules

Whether you're traveling for 7 days or 70, pack for one week. You'll do laundry. Hotels have laundry services. Airbnbs have washers. Sink washing works for underwear and light fabrics. The math is simple: 7 underwear, 7 tops (some lightweight), 2-3 bottoms, done.

2Ô∏è‚É£ One Color Palette, Everything Mixes

Pick a base (black, navy, or olive works best), add a neutral (white, cream, gray), then 1-2 accent colors. Every top should work with every bottom. This isn't boring‚Äîit's freedom. You'll look intentional, not like you packed randomly.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Weigh Your "Just in Case" Items

For every "just in case" item, ask: can I buy this there if I actually need it? The answer is almost always yes. That book you might read? Get an e-reader. That backup outfit? You won't wear it. Those extra shoes? One pair for walking, one for nicer occasions, max.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Your Bag Should Be Comfortable Empty

If your bag is heavy before you put anything in it, it's the wrong bag. A good travel backpack or carry-on weighs under 3 lbs empty. Skip the "features" that add weight‚Äîyou want simplicity and durability.

What to Leave Behind

These are the biggest space-wasters I see:

  • "Going out" heels ‚Äî You won't wear them. Flats or wedges work everywhere.
  • Full-size toiletries ‚Äî Hotels provide basics. Buy what you need there.
  • Bulky towels ‚Äî A microfiber travel towel is half the size and dries in hours.
  • Multiple books ‚Äî E-reader or trade books as you go.
  • Valuables you'd cry over losing ‚Äî Leave the expensive jewelry at home.
  • Hair tools ‚Äî Learn to love your natural texture or find a salon.
  • More than one bag ‚Äî Cross-body for daily use. That's it.

The Right Bag Matters

For Backpackers: 35-40L Backpack

Look for: hip belt (distributes weight), laptop sleeve, multiple access points, and a design that doesn't scream "tourist." Recommended: Osprey Fairview 40, Cotopaxi Allpa 35, Peak Design Travel Backpack.

For Carry-On Travelers: Spinner Suitcase

If you're sticking to developed destinations with good infrastructure, a spinner carry-on works beautifully. Look for: lightweight shell, smooth wheels, good organization. Recommended: Away Carry-On, Monos Carry-On, Samsonite Freeform.

The Freedom of Less

When you pack light, you move through the world differently. You're faster, more flexible, and more confident. You can change plans easily, take spontaneous trains, and never worry about lost luggage. Start with one trip‚Äîyou'll never go back.
