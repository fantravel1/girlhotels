Why Women Love This Hotel

The Marlton is Greenwich Village distilled into 107 rooms. This 1900 building once housed Jack Kerouac during his early writing years and Lenny Bruce during his New York days. Now it's a jewel box of French-inspired design: herringbone floors, brass fixtures, and rooms that feel like a glamorous Parisian apartment. The ground-floor Margaux restaurant is perfect for solo dining at the bar. The location on West 8th Street puts you steps from Washington Square Park, the best bookshops, jazz clubs, and the energy that makes the Village legendary. For solo women, the Marlton offers style, safety, and a neighborhood built for wandering.
